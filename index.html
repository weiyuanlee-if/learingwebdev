WILFREDO LAM

林飛龍

Wilfredo Lam was an Afro-Chinese Cuban painter known for his surrealist African art that would make him an influential figure in the Cubism movement and eventually becoming the head of an Afro-Cuban revolution that lives on for generations to come. His works often depicts African life and spirituality as an act of empowerment against colonialism. You can find all of his art pieces displayed on the auction and art database, artnet.

  • 1902 - Born in Sagua la Grande, Cuba to an Afro-Cuban mother and a Chinese immigrant father.
  • 1916 - Lam's family settled in Havana, where he enrolled in Escuela Profesional de Pintura y Escultura, Academia de San Alejandro.
  • 1923 - He won a grant to study art in Europe and left for Spain to study art for 14 years.
  • 1931 - He married Eva Sébastiana Piriz and had a son, Wilfredo Victor. They both died of tuberculosis.
  • 1938 - Lam left for Paris and would soon meet Picasso and have his first art exhibit.
  • 1941 - He returned to Cuba and met Negritude movement poet, Aimé Césaire.
  • 1942 - Lam created a multitude of art pieces, including his most famous, La Jungula.
  • 1958 - He won the Grand Prix of the Havana Salon and becomes a member of the Graham Foundation for Advanced Study in Fine Art.
  • 1964 - He wins the Guggenheim International Award and he would travel between France and Italy.
  • 1982 - Lam passed at 79 years old.
